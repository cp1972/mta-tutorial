Q: Did you find the restrictions justified or excessive?
R: Streaming services kept us entertained through the long evenings indoors. The shift to remote work was difficult at my age, the technology was new.

Q: How did you experience the lockdown periods?
R: I noticed I made fewer but more meaningful contacts during that time. I had to learn many new digital tools faster than I would have liked. The mental health consequences are still with us, I think, even now.

Q: What changed the most in your daily life during the pandemic?
R: I started paying much more attention to my general health and immunity. My partner and I both lost income at the same time, it was hard.

Q: Did you worry about your health or the health of people close to you?
R: I had to learn to lead meetings on video, it changed my management style. I learned who my real friends were when we could not meet in person. Society proved more fragile than I thought but also more resilient.

Q: How did you feel about masks and physical distancing rules?
R: Knowing someone in intensive care really brought the threat home. I had to learn to lead meetings on video, it changed my management style. Many of my friends were in the same boat, all our plans got postponed. Paying rent became a real source of anxiety, every month was a battle.

Q: How did you handle work or income during this period?
R: Video calls replaced almost all face-to-face interaction for months. I noticed I made fewer but more meaningful contacts during that time. I got vaccinated as soon as I was eligible, it gave me real peace of mind. Internships were cancelled, it really delayed the start of my career.

Q: How do you look back on the vaccination campaign?
R: The mask debates in the media were exhausting to follow. Wearing a mask became second nature after a few weeks.
