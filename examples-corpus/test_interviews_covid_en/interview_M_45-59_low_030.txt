Q: What role did digital tools play in your daily life?
R: I got vaccinated as soon as I was eligible, it gave me real peace of mind. I postponed dental care and other things because money was scarce. I had to learn many new digital tools faster than I would have liked.

Q: What lessons do you take from this period?
R: Money was very tight during those months, I had to cut everything. The eviction moratorium probably saved us from being homeless. I had a sore arm and tiredness for a day, but nothing worse than that. I lost my job during the first wave and finding a new one took ages.

Q: How did you experience the lockdown periods?
R: I joined virtual social events, which felt strange but better than nothing. Restructuring at my company put older employees under particular pressure.

Q: How did you feel about masks and physical distancing rules?
R: The mask debates in the media were exhausting to follow. I appreciate small everyday freedoms much more than I used to.

Q: How did you handle work or income during this period?
R: I rediscovered email and phone calls after years of mostly messaging. Unemployment benefits arrived late and were not enough to live on. I had to learn many new digital tools faster than I would have liked.

Q: How did your social relationships evolve during the pandemic?
R: School closures forced us to homeschool while keeping our jobs going. I went to the food bank for the first time in my life, it was humbling. Coordinating projects across distributed teams was a real shift.

Q: Did you worry about your health or the health of people close to you?
R: I had to ask my family for help with the bills, which I had never done. Every time someone in the family coughed, the anxiety came back. I appreciate small everyday freedoms much more than I used to. I had a sore arm and tiredness for a day, but nothing worse than that.
