Q: How did you experience the lockdown periods?
R: I felt the restrictions were generally reasonable given the circumstances. Outdoor walks with one friend at a time became precious moments.

Q: What lessons do you take from this period?
R: My partner and I both lost income at the same time, it was hard. Reading the daily case numbers became an unhealthy obsession for a while. The mental health consequences are still with us, I think, even now.

Q: What changed the most in your daily life during the pandemic?
R: Reading the daily case numbers became an unhealthy obsession for a while. Internships were cancelled, it really delayed the start of my career.

Q: How did you feel about masks and physical distancing rules?
R: Internships were cancelled, it really delayed the start of my career. Online grocery shopping became a habit I have kept ever since.

Q: How did your social relationships evolve during the pandemic?
R: I moved back in with my parents because I could not afford rent alone. Every time someone in the family coughed, the anxiety came back. I started paying much more attention to my general health and immunity.

Q: Did you find the restrictions justified or excessive?
R: Most of the housework fell on me even though we were both home. I postponed dental care and other things because money was scarce. I learned who my real friends were when we could not meet in person. I went to the food bank for the first time in my life, it was humbling.

Q: How did you handle work or income during this period?
R: Some things changed permanently, like how we view work and where we live. I rediscovered email and phone calls after years of mostly messaging. Reading the daily case numbers became an unhealthy obsession for a while. I lost my job during the first wave and finding a new one took ages.
