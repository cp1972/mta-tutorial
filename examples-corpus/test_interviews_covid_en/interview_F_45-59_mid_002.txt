Q: How did you feel about masks and physical distancing rules?
R: I think governments learned a lot but communicated badly at the start. I used some of our savings to get through the uncertain months.

Q: Did you worry about your health or the health of people close to you?
R: I waited a few weeks to see how the vaccines worked before taking mine. Childcare and household tasks combined left no time for myself. Keeping physical distance from people felt strange and unnatural at first.

Q: How did you experience the lockdown periods?
R: Online grocery shopping became a habit I have kept ever since. We postponed buying a new car and a few other expenses. The shift to remote work was difficult at my age, the technology was new.

Q: What lessons do you take from this period?
R: I learned who my real friends were when we could not meet in person. Some people in my circle were hesitant, we had many long conversations. We postponed buying a new car and a few other expenses.

Q: What role did digital tools play in your daily life?
R: At my age, the prospect of losing my job and finding another was scary. I worried that age discrimination would make a job search impossible. I was constantly cooking, the family ate every meal at home for months. I noticed I was the one keeping track of supplies, medicines, schedules.

Q: How do you look back on the vaccination campaign?
R: At my age, the prospect of losing my job and finding another was scary. Childcare and household tasks combined left no time for myself. The mortgage was a worry but we kept up with payments.

Q: What changed the most in your daily life during the pandemic?
R: Some things changed permanently, like how we view work and where we live. We postponed buying a new car and a few other expenses.
