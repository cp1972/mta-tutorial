Q: How did you experience the lockdown periods?
R: We bought a second property in the countryside for weekends away. Streaming services kept us entertained through the long evenings indoors. I noticed I was the one keeping track of supplies, medicines, schedules.

Q: What changed the most in your daily life during the pandemic?
R: Looking back, the response could have been faster and more coordinated. I felt my early career years were stolen by the pandemic. The mortgage was a worry but we kept up with payments. I worried that age discrimination would make a job search impossible.

Q: How do you look back on the vaccination campaign?
R: My salary was reduced for a while but my job was never really at risk. I used some of our savings to get through the uncertain months. I started paying much more attention to my general health and immunity. At my age, the prospect of losing my job and finding another was scary.

Q: Did you find the restrictions justified or excessive?
R: Society proved more fragile than I thought but also more resilient. My parents were elderly and I lived in fear of bringing the virus home.

Q: How did you feel about masks and physical distancing rules?
R: I used some of our savings to get through the uncertain months. Childcare and household tasks combined left no time for myself.

Q: How did you handle work or income during this period?
R: Most of the housework fell on me even though we were both home. Travel restrictions cost us nothing financially, we just postponed trips. I redesigned my workflow around asynchronous communication tools. I sometimes still wear a mask in crowded places out of habit.

Q: Did you worry about your health or the health of people close to you?
R: I postponed dental care and other things because money was scarce. I went weeks without seeing anyone in person, only voices on the phone. Reading the daily case numbers became an unhealthy obsession for a while.
