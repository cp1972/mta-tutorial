Q: How did your social relationships evolve during the pandemic?
R: I waited a few weeks to see how the vaccines worked before taking mine. Outdoor walks with one friend at a time became precious moments.

Q: How did you feel about masks and physical distancing rules?
R: My parents were elderly and I lived in fear of bringing the virus home. The mask debates in the media were exhausting to follow. Reconnecting with people after restrictions lifted was sometimes awkward. I had complicated feelings about masking children in schools.

Q: What role did digital tools play in your daily life?
R: I sometimes still wear a mask in crowded places out of habit. We bought a second property in the countryside for weekends away.

Q: How did you experience the lockdown periods?
R: I joined virtual social events, which felt strange but better than nothing. The kids missed their friends terribly and it broke my heart to see. Reconnecting with people after restrictions lifted was sometimes awkward. I got vaccinated as soon as I was eligible, it gave me real peace of mind.

Q: Did you find the restrictions justified or excessive?
R: I was constantly worried about catching the virus and falling severely ill. The mortgage was a worry but we kept up with payments.

Q: What lessons do you take from this period?
R: The shift to remote work was difficult at my age, the technology was new. Helping with homework on top of my own work was completely draining.

Q: What changed the most in your daily life during the pandemic?
R: Reading the daily case numbers became an unhealthy obsession for a while. Wearing a mask became second nature after a few weeks. The mortgage was a worry but we kept up with payments.
