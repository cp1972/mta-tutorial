Q: How did you handle work or income during this period?
R: The emotional support for the whole family became my unspoken job. I used some of our savings to get through the uncertain months.

Q: Did you worry about your health or the health of people close to you?
R: Childcare and household tasks combined left no time for myself. I felt the restrictions were generally reasonable given the circumstances. Reading the daily case numbers became an unhealthy obsession for a while.

Q: How did you experience the lockdown periods?
R: The mask debates in the media were exhausting to follow. Productivity actually improved without commuting and office distractions.

Q: How did your social relationships evolve during the pandemic?
R: Childcare and household tasks combined left no time for myself. The isolation was harder than the fear of the illness itself.

Q: What role did digital tools play in your daily life?
R: I started paying much more attention to my general health and immunity. I waited a few weeks to see how the vaccines worked before taking mine. I appreciate small everyday freedoms much more than I used to. Keeping physical distance from people felt strange and unnatural at first.

Q: What lessons do you take from this period?
R: Every time someone in the family coughed, the anxiety came back. Knowing someone in intensive care really brought the threat home. The isolation was harder than the fear of the illness itself.

Q: What changed the most in your daily life during the pandemic?
R: I think governments learned a lot but communicated badly at the start. Family video calls became the highlight of my week. Looking back, the response could have been faster and more coordinated. Online grocery shopping became a habit I have kept ever since.
