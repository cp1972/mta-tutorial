Q: Did you find the restrictions justified or excessive?
R: The shift to remote work was difficult at my age, the technology was new. We postponed buying a new car and a few other expenses. I was constantly cooking, the family ate every meal at home for months.

Q: How did you handle work or income during this period?
R: School closures forced us to homeschool while keeping our jobs going. Some people in my circle were hesitant, we had many long conversations. Video calls replaced almost all face-to-face interaction for months. The emotional support for the whole family became my unspoken job.

Q: Did you worry about your health or the health of people close to you?
R: Most of the housework fell on me even though we were both home. I was constantly worried about catching the virus and falling severely ill. The emotional support for the whole family became my unspoken job. The vaccination process was well organized in my area, very efficient.

Q: What changed the most in your daily life during the pandemic?
R: I think the pandemic forced us to reconsider what really matters in life. I felt the restrictions were generally reasonable given the circumstances. The mental load of organizing the household exploded during lockdown. At my age, the prospect of losing my job and finding another was scary.

Q: How did your social relationships evolve during the pandemic?
R: I trusted the public health authorities even when their advice changed. Setting up the home office, the VPN, the dual monitors took some work. My salary was reduced for a while but my job was never really at risk. Paying rent became a real source of anxiety, every month was a battle.

Q: What role did digital tools play in your daily life?
R: We postponed buying a new car and a few other expenses. I think governments learned a lot but communicated badly at the start. I used some of our savings to get through the uncertain months. Coordinating projects across distributed teams was a real shift.

Q: How did you feel about masks and physical distancing rules?
R: I think governments learned a lot but communicated badly at the start. My grandchildren visited through the window or on a video call. Video calls replaced almost all face-to-face interaction for months. I noticed I was the one keeping track of supplies, medicines, schedules.
