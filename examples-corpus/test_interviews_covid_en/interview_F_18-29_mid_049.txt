R: Some relationships deepened, others quietly faded during the long months. I was a student then and my part-time job disappeared overnight. I noticed I was the one keeping track of supplies, medicines, schedules. I learned who my real friends were when we could not meet in person.

Q: How did you experience the lockdown periods?
R: I learned who my real friends were when we could not meet in person. Online grocery shopping became a habit I have kept ever since. Many of my friends were in the same boat, all our plans got postponed.

Q: What lessons do you take from this period?
R: I had a sore arm and tiredness for a day, but nothing worse than that. I used some of our savings to get through the uncertain months. Online grocery shopping became a habit I have kept ever since. Reconnecting with people after restrictions lifted was sometimes awkward.

Q: How did your social relationships evolve during the pandemic?
R: I had complicated feelings about masking children in schools. I sometimes still wear a mask in crowded places out of habit. I appreciate small everyday freedoms much more than I used to.

Q: What changed the most in your daily life during the pandemic?
R: I had complicated feelings about masking children in schools. I think the pandemic forced us to reconsider what really matters in life. We postponed buying a new car and a few other expenses.

Q: Did you worry about your health or the health of people close to you?
R: Some things changed permanently, like how we view work and where we live. Every time someone in the family coughed, the anxiety came back.
