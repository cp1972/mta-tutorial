Q: How did you feel about masks and physical distancing rules?
R: Society proved more fragile than I thought but also more resilient. The mask debates in the media were exhausting to follow.

Q: How did you experience the lockdown periods?
R: The emotional support for the whole family became my unspoken job. School closures forced us to homeschool while keeping our jobs going.

Q: How did you handle work or income during this period?
R: The mental load of organizing the household exploded during lockdown. We bought tablets for each child so they could attend online classes.

Q: Did you worry about your health or the health of people close to you?
R: We even saved money during lockdown since we could not travel or eat out. Helping with homework on top of my own work was completely draining. I worked from our holiday home for several months, it was actually pleasant. I learned who my real friends were when we could not meet in person.

Q: How did your social relationships evolve during the pandemic?
R: I noticed I made fewer but more meaningful contacts during that time. I was constantly cooking, the family ate every meal at home for months. Childcare and household tasks combined left no time for myself. Knowing someone in intensive care really brought the threat home.

Q: How do you look back on the vaccination campaign?
R: The mortgage was a worry but we kept up with payments. We bought a second property in the countryside for weekends away.

Q: What lessons do you take from this period?
R: I noticed I made fewer but more meaningful contacts during that time. We supported local businesses since we had the means to do so. Online grocery shopping became a habit I have kept ever since.
