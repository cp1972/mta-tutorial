Q: What role did digital tools play in your daily life?
R: My grandchildren visited through the window or on a video call. I appreciate small everyday freedoms much more than I used to.

Q: How did you handle work or income during this period?
R: I used some of our savings to get through the uncertain months. I had to ask my family for help with the bills, which I had never done. I learned who my real friends were when we could not meet in person.

Q: What changed the most in your daily life during the pandemic?
R: I rediscovered email and phone calls after years of mostly messaging. Unemployment benefits arrived late and were not enough to live on. I trusted the public health authorities even when their advice changed. I postponed dental care and other things because money was scarce.

Q: How did you feel about masks and physical distancing rules?
R: The eviction moratorium probably saved us from being homeless. Online grocery shopping became a habit I have kept ever since.

Q: How do you look back on the vaccination campaign?
R: Coordinating projects across distributed teams was a real shift. The vaccine appointment day was a real relief, almost a celebration. The mental health consequences are still with us, I think, even now. I felt forgotten by the world during those long quiet months.

Q: Did you worry about your health or the health of people close to you?
R: Productivity actually improved without commuting and office distractions. Society proved more fragile than I thought but also more resilient.
