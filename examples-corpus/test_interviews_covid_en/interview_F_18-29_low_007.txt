Q: How do you look back on the vaccination campaign?
R: I lost my job during the first wave and finding a new one took ages. Many of my friends were in the same boat, all our plans got postponed. Paying rent became a real source of anxiety, every month was a battle. Every time someone in the family coughed, the anxiety came back.

Q: What role did digital tools play in your daily life?
R: Money was very tight during those months, I had to cut everything. Graduation ceremonies, parties, all the milestones were just gone.

Q: What lessons do you take from this period?
R: I learned who my real friends were when we could not meet in person. Childcare and household tasks combined left no time for myself. We postponed buying a new car and a few other expenses. I was constantly cooking, the family ate every meal at home for months.

Q: Did you worry about your health or the health of people close to you?
R: I was constantly worried about catching the virus and falling severely ill. I felt the restrictions were generally reasonable given the circumstances.

Q: What changed the most in your daily life during the pandemic?
