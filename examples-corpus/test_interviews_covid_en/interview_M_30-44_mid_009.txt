Q: Did you find the restrictions justified or excessive?
R: Coordinating projects across distributed teams was a real shift. My salary was reduced for a while but my job was never really at risk. I worried that age discrimination would make a job search impossible. Productivity actually improved without commuting and office distractions.

Q: How did you experience the lockdown periods?
R: We bought tablets for each child so they could attend online classes. School closures forced us to homeschool while keeping our jobs going. I redesigned my workflow around asynchronous communication tools.

Q: How do you look back on the vaccination campaign?
R: I appreciate small everyday freedoms much more than I used to. School closures forced us to homeschool while keeping our jobs going.

Q: How did your social relationships evolve during the pandemic?
R: We managed financially but had to be more careful with spending. Some things changed permanently, like how we view work and where we live. Reconnecting with people after restrictions lifted was sometimes awkward. I used some of our savings to get through the uncertain months.

Q: What lessons do you take from this period?
R: Booster doses became part of the routine, like an annual habit. Society proved more fragile than I thought but also more resilient. I started paying much more attention to my general health and immunity.

Q: How did you feel about masks and physical distancing rules?
R: Wearing a mask became second nature after a few weeks. Online grocery shopping became a habit I have kept ever since.
