Q: What role did digital tools play in your daily life?
R: We bought tablets for each child so they could attend online classes. I had to learn to lead meetings on video, it changed my management style.

Q: How did your social relationships evolve during the pandemic?
R: Coordinating projects across distributed teams was a real shift. Setting up the home office, the VPN, the dual monitors took some work. I used some of our savings to get through the uncertain months. I was constantly cooking, the family ate every meal at home for months.

Q: Did you find the restrictions justified or excessive?
R: The vaccination process was well organized in my area, very efficient. I redesigned my workflow around asynchronous communication tools. Helping with homework on top of my own work was completely draining.

Q: What lessons do you take from this period?
R: I started paying much more attention to my general health and immunity. Daycare closures meant we juggled meetings and toddlers all day. I used some of our savings to get through the uncertain months.

Q: How did you experience the lockdown periods?
R: The mortgage was a worry but we kept up with payments. Some things changed permanently, like how we view work and where we live. Graduation ceremonies, parties, all the milestones were just gone.

Q: How do you look back on the vaccination campaign?
R: Reading the daily case numbers became an unhealthy obsession for a while. Wearing a mask became second nature after a few weeks. Outdoor walks with one friend at a time became precious moments.

Q: What changed the most in your daily life during the pandemic?
R: My parents were elderly and I lived in fear of bringing the virus home. My partner and I both lost income at the same time, it was hard.
