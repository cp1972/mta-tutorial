Q: What lessons do you take from this period?
R: Wearing a mask became second nature after a few weeks. I had to learn many new digital tools faster than I would have liked.

Q: How did you handle work or income during this period?
R: The vaccination process was well organized in my area, very efficient. The emotional support for the whole family became my unspoken job. At my age, the prospect of losing my job and finding another was scary.

Q: How did your social relationships evolve during the pandemic?
R: We cut spending on food and clothes to keep the essentials going. I sometimes still wear a mask in crowded places out of habit. Reconnecting with people after restrictions lifted was sometimes awkward. The shift to remote work was difficult at my age, the technology was new.

Q: What role did digital tools play in your daily life?
R: Productivity actually improved without commuting and office distractions. Outdoor walks with one friend at a time became precious moments.

Q: Did you worry about your health or the health of people close to you?
R: I redesigned my workflow around asynchronous communication tools. Outdoor walks with one friend at a time became precious moments. I felt the restrictions were generally reasonable given the circumstances. We cut spending on food and clothes to keep the essentials going.

Q: How do you look back on the vaccination campaign?
R: I went to the food bank for the first time in my life, it was humbling. Most of the housework fell on me even though we were both home.

Q: How did you feel about masks and physical distancing rules?
R: I used the savings to renovate our house and improve our home office. I think governments learned a lot but communicated badly at the start. I had to ask my family for help with the bills, which I had never done.
