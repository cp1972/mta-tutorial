Q: Did you worry about your health or the health of people close to you?
R: Setting up the home office, the VPN, the dual monitors took some work. Outdoor walks with one friend at a time became precious moments. I was constantly worried about catching the virus and falling severely ill. I sometimes still wear a mask in crowded places out of habit.

Q: What changed the most in your daily life during the pandemic?
R: Some relationships deepened, others quietly faded during the long months. I rediscovered email and phone calls after years of mostly messaging. I moved back in with my parents because I could not afford rent alone.

Q: How did you handle work or income during this period?
R: Graduation ceremonies, parties, all the milestones were just gone. My partner and I both lost income at the same time, it was hard.

Q: How do you look back on the vaccination campaign?
R: Online classes were exhausting and not what I had signed up for. I got vaccinated as soon as I was eligible, it gave me real peace of mind. I postponed dental care and other things because money was scarce. Society proved more fragile than I thought but also more resilient.

Q: How did you experience the lockdown periods?
R: The emotional support for the whole family became my unspoken job. I learned who my real friends were when we could not meet in person. Unemployment benefits arrived late and were not enough to live on. I had a sore arm and tiredness for a day, but nothing worse than that.

Q: How did you feel about masks and physical distancing rules?
R: I started paying much more attention to my general health and immunity. My partner and I both lost income at the same time, it was hard. I waited a few weeks to see how the vaccines worked before taking mine.

Q: Did you find the restrictions justified or excessive?
R: Setting up the home office, the VPN, the dual monitors took some work. Reconnecting with people after restrictions lifted was sometimes awkward.
