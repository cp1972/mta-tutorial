Q: How do you look back on the vaccination campaign?
R: Online grocery shopping became a habit I have kept ever since. Setting up the home office, the VPN, the dual monitors took some work. The mortgage was a worry but we kept up with payments. I went weeks without seeing anyone in person, only voices on the phone.

Q: How did you experience the lockdown periods?
R: We postponed buying a new car and a few other expenses. I went to the food bank for the first time in my life, it was humbling. I had to learn to lead meetings on video, it changed my management style.

Q: What role did digital tools play in your daily life?
R: Productivity actually improved without commuting and office distractions. Family video calls became the highlight of my week.

Q: What lessons do you take from this period?
R: My salary was reduced for a while but my job was never really at risk. Reconnecting with people after restrictions lifted was sometimes awkward. The isolation was harder than the fear of the illness itself. I had a sore arm and tiredness for a day, but nothing worse than that.

Q: Did you worry about your health or the health of people close to you?
R: We even saved money during lockdown since we could not travel or eat out. I lost a friend to the virus and could not attend the funeral properly. I think the pandemic forced us to reconsider what really matters in life. Setting up the home office, the VPN, the dual monitors took some work.

Q: How did you feel about masks and physical distancing rules?
R: I was constantly worried about catching the virus and falling severely ill. Productivity actually improved without commuting and office distractions. I felt the restrictions were generally reasonable given the circumstances. I was advised to stay home for months because of my age and health.
