Q: How did your social relationships evolve during the pandemic?
R: I noticed I made fewer but more meaningful contacts during that time. School closures forced us to homeschool while keeping our jobs going.

Q: What changed the most in your daily life during the pandemic?
R: We cut spending on food and clothes to keep the essentials going. Keeping physical distance from people felt strange and unnatural at first. Unemployment benefits arrived late and were not enough to live on.

Q: What role did digital tools play in your daily life?
R: I had to learn to lead meetings on video, it changed my management style. I postponed dental care and other things because money was scarce. Reconnecting with people after restrictions lifted was sometimes awkward.

Q: How did you handle work or income during this period?
R: I lost my job during the first wave and finding a new one took ages. I went to the food bank for the first time in my life, it was humbling. I had to learn to lead meetings on video, it changed my management style.

Q: Did you worry about your health or the health of people close to you?
R: Daycare closures meant we juggled meetings and toddlers all day. Productivity actually improved without commuting and office distractions.

Q: How did you feel about masks and physical distancing rules?
R: Working from home with the kids was an enormous challenge daily. Helping with homework on top of my own work was completely draining. I got vaccinated as soon as I was eligible, it gave me real peace of mind.

Q: What lessons do you take from this period?
R: I had to ask my family for help with the bills, which I had never done. My partner and I both lost income at the same time, it was hard. I think governments learned a lot but communicated badly at the start. Some people in my circle were hesitant, we had many long conversations.
