Q: Did you worry about your health or the health of people close to you?
R: We postponed buying a new car and a few other expenses. I noticed I made fewer but more meaningful contacts during that time.

Q: What lessons do you take from this period?
R: Some things changed permanently, like how we view work and where we live. Reconnecting with people after restrictions lifted was sometimes awkward.

Q: What changed the most in your daily life during the pandemic?
R: Online classes were exhausting and not what I had signed up for. I redesigned my workflow around asynchronous communication tools. Productivity actually improved without commuting and office distractions. Setting up the home office, the VPN, the dual monitors took some work.

Q: How did you handle work or income during this period?
R: I had to learn to lead meetings on video, it changed my management style. Many of my friends were in the same boat, all our plans got postponed. My salary was reduced for a while but my job was never really at risk.

Q: How did you feel about masks and physical distancing rules?
R: Some people in my circle were hesitant, we had many long conversations. I started paying much more attention to my general health and immunity. Wearing a mask became second nature after a few weeks.

Q: Did you find the restrictions justified or excessive?
R: My salary was reduced for a while but my job was never really at risk. Streaming services kept us entertained through the long evenings indoors. The shift to remote work was difficult at my age, the technology was new.

Q: How did your social relationships evolve during the pandemic?
R: Keeping physical distance from people felt strange and unnatural at first. My partner and I had to split the day in shifts to manage the children.
