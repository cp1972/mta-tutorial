Q: What role did digital tools play in your daily life?
R: The mental health consequences are still with us, I think, even now. Restructuring at my company put older employees under particular pressure. Wearing a mask became second nature after a few weeks.

Q: How did you handle work or income during this period?
R: The pandemic barely affected our family budget, we were fortunate. Every time someone in the family coughed, the anxiety came back. The vaccine appointment day was a real relief, almost a celebration. I felt forgotten by the world during those long quiet months.

Q: What changed the most in your daily life during the pandemic?
R: I went weeks without seeing anyone in person, only voices on the phone. My parents were elderly and I lived in fear of bringing the virus home. The vaccination process was well organized in my area, very efficient.

Q: What lessons do you take from this period?
R: I had a sore arm and tiredness for a day, but nothing worse than that. We even saved money during lockdown since we could not travel or eat out.

Q: Did you find the restrictions justified or excessive?
R: The isolation was harder than the fear of the illness itself. Reconnecting with people after restrictions lifted was sometimes awkward.

Q: How did your social relationships evolve during the pandemic?
R: I had complicated feelings about masking children in schools. Setting up the home office, the VPN, the dual monitors took some work.

Q: How did you feel about masks and physical distancing rules?
R: I had to learn many new digital tools faster than I would have liked. Looking back, the response could have been faster and more coordinated. I learned who my real friends were when we could not meet in person.
