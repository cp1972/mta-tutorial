Q: How did you handle work or income during this period?
R: I was constantly cooking, the family ate every meal at home for months. Video calls replaced almost all face-to-face interaction for months. Knowing someone in intensive care really brought the threat home. Paying rent became a real source of anxiety, every month was a battle.

Q: How did you experience the lockdown periods?
R: Video calls replaced almost all face-to-face interaction for months. My partner and I had to split the day in shifts to manage the children.

Q: What role did digital tools play in your daily life?
R: Restructuring at my company put older employees under particular pressure. Most of the housework fell on me even though we were both home. Every time someone in the family coughed, the anxiety came back. Working from home with the kids was an enormous challenge daily.

Q: How did your social relationships evolve during the pandemic?
R: School closures forced us to homeschool while keeping our jobs going. Streaming services kept us entertained through the long evenings indoors. I noticed I made fewer but more meaningful contacts during that time. Looking back, the response could have been faster and more coordinated.

Q: How did you feel about masks and physical distancing rules?
R: Society proved more fragile than I thought but also more resilient. The vaccination process was well organized in my area, very efficient. I trusted the public health authorities even when their advice changed.

Q: What changed the most in your daily life during the pandemic?
R: Streaming services kept us entertained through the long evenings indoors. The eviction moratorium probably saved us from being homeless. Some things changed permanently, like how we view work and where we live.

Q: Did you worry about your health or the health of people close to you?
