Q: What changed the most in your daily life during the pandemic?
R: The emotional support for the whole family became my unspoken job. We postponed buying a new car and a few other expenses. Job interviews on video felt strange, I struggled to make a connection. We managed financially but had to be more careful with spending.

Q: How did you handle work or income during this period?
R: I learned who my real friends were when we could not meet in person. Every time someone in the family coughed, the anxiety came back.

Q: What role did digital tools play in your daily life?
R: Online classes were exhausting and not what I had signed up for. I sometimes still wear a mask in crowded places out of habit. The mask debates in the media were exhausting to follow. I felt my early career years were stolen by the pandemic.

Q: Did you find the restrictions justified or excessive?
R: I felt my early career years were stolen by the pandemic. Childcare and household tasks combined left no time for myself. I noticed I made fewer but more meaningful contacts during that time. I had a sore arm and tiredness for a day, but nothing worse than that.

Q: How do you look back on the vaccination campaign?
R: Every time someone in the family coughed, the anxiety came back. Internships were cancelled, it really delayed the start of my career.

Q: Did you worry about your health or the health of people close to you?
R: The mortgage was a worry but we kept up with payments. Many of my friends were in the same boat, all our plans got postponed. Internships were cancelled, it really delayed the start of my career. Graduation ceremonies, parties, all the milestones were just gone.

Q: How did you feel about masks and physical distancing rules?
R: I appreciate small everyday freedoms much more than I used to. Booster doses became part of the routine, like an annual habit. Most of the housework fell on me even though we were both home. The mental load of organizing the household exploded during lockdown.
