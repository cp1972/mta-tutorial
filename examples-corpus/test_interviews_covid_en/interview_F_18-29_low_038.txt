Q: How did your social relationships evolve during the pandemic?
R: I rediscovered email and phone calls after years of mostly messaging. I had to learn many new digital tools faster than I would have liked. The mask debates in the media were exhausting to follow.

Q: What role did digital tools play in your daily life?
R: Online grocery shopping became a habit I have kept ever since. I rediscovered email and phone calls after years of mostly messaging. I learned who my real friends were when we could not meet in person. I redesigned my workflow around asynchronous communication tools.

Q: Did you worry about your health or the health of people close to you?
R: Internships were cancelled, it really delayed the start of my career. My partner and I both lost income at the same time, it was hard. Some rules seemed inconsistent and that undermined public trust. Keeping physical distance from people felt strange and unnatural at first.

Q: What changed the most in your daily life during the pandemic?
R: Graduation ceremonies, parties, all the milestones were just gone. Paying rent became a real source of anxiety, every month was a battle.

Q: How do you look back on the vaccination campaign?
R: Wearing a mask became second nature after a few weeks. Looking back, the response could have been faster and more coordinated. I noticed I made fewer but more meaningful contacts during that time. I postponed dental care and other things because money was scarce.

Q: What lessons do you take from this period?
R: I had complicated feelings about masking children in schools. I noticed I was the one keeping track of supplies, medicines, schedules. Streaming services kept us entertained through the long evenings indoors.

Q: Did you find the restrictions justified or excessive?
R: Some relationships deepened, others quietly faded during the long months. Wearing a mask became second nature after a few weeks. Most of the housework fell on me even though we were both home.
