Q: What lessons do you take from this period?
R: Graduation ceremonies, parties, all the milestones were just gone. My salary was reduced for a while but my job was never really at risk. Many of my friends were in the same boat, all our plans got postponed. Internships were cancelled, it really delayed the start of my career.

Q: How did your social relationships evolve during the pandemic?
R: We postponed buying a new car and a few other expenses. Every time someone in the family coughed, the anxiety came back.

Q: Did you worry about your health or the health of people close to you?
R: I noticed I was the one keeping track of supplies, medicines, schedules. The mask debates in the media were exhausting to follow.

Q: How do you look back on the vaccination campaign?
R: I felt my early career years were stolen by the pandemic. Childcare and household tasks combined left no time for myself.

Q: What role did digital tools play in your daily life?
R: The eviction moratorium probably saved us from being homeless. Most of the housework fell on me even though we were both home. I think governments learned a lot but communicated badly at the start. Streaming services kept us entertained through the long evenings indoors.
