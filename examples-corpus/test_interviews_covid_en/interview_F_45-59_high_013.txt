Q: What changed the most in your daily life during the pandemic?
R: I felt forgotten by the world during those long quiet months. I worried that age discrimination would make a job search impossible.

Q: How did you handle work or income during this period?
R: Knowing someone in intensive care really brought the threat home. Our investments took a hit at first but recovered nicely afterwards. I trusted the public health authorities even when their advice changed.

Q: Did you worry about your health or the health of people close to you?
R: Online grocery shopping became a habit I have kept ever since. Childcare and household tasks combined left no time for myself. The kids missed their friends terribly and it broke my heart to see. Some rules seemed inconsistent and that undermined public trust.

Q: How did you feel about masks and physical distancing rules?
R: We bought a second property in the countryside for weekends away. I had complicated feelings about masking children in schools.

Q: How did your social relationships evolve during the pandemic?
R: The emotional support for the whole family became my unspoken job. Some people in my circle were hesitant, we had many long conversations. I lost a friend to the virus and could not attend the funeral properly.

Q: How did you experience the lockdown periods?
R: The emotional support for the whole family became my unspoken job. Financially we were lucky, my job continued from home without issue. I think governments learned a lot but communicated badly at the start. Wearing a mask became second nature after a few weeks.

Q: How do you look back on the vaccination campaign?
R: Working from home with the kids was an enormous challenge daily. I appreciate small everyday freedoms much more than I used to.
