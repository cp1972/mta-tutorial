Q: Did you worry about your health or the health of people close to you?
R: The mental health consequences are still with us, I think, even now. The kids missed their friends terribly and it broke my heart to see.

Q: What role did digital tools play in your daily life?
R: I got vaccinated as soon as I was eligible, it gave me real peace of mind. Unemployment benefits arrived late and were not enough to live on.

Q: How did you handle work or income during this period?
R: Wearing a mask became second nature after a few weeks. Money was very tight during those months, I had to cut everything.

Q: How do you look back on the vaccination campaign?
R: Reading the daily case numbers became an unhealthy obsession for a while. I lost my job during the first wave and finding a new one took ages.

Q: How did your social relationships evolve during the pandemic?
R: I noticed I was the one keeping track of supplies, medicines, schedules. Most of the housework fell on me even though we were both home. I was constantly cooking, the family ate every meal at home for months.

Q: How did you experience the lockdown periods?
R: My partner and I both lost income at the same time, it was hard. I had to learn many new digital tools faster than I would have liked. I think the pandemic forced us to reconsider what really matters in life. Society proved more fragile than I thought but also more resilient.

Q: What lessons do you take from this period?
R: I had a sore arm and tiredness for a day, but nothing worse than that. Knowing someone in intensive care really brought the threat home. I lost my job during the first wave and finding a new one took ages.
