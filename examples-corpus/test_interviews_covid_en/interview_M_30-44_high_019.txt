Q: What changed the most in your daily life during the pandemic?
R: Some people in my circle were hesitant, we had many long conversations. Booster doses became part of the routine, like an annual habit. I was constantly cooking, the family ate every meal at home for months.

Q: How did you feel about masks and physical distancing rules?
R: I had a sore arm and tiredness for a day, but nothing worse than that. I sometimes still wear a mask in crowded places out of habit. Travel restrictions cost us nothing financially, we just postponed trips.

Q: Did you worry about your health or the health of people close to you?
R: I had to learn to lead meetings on video, it changed my management style. I learned who my real friends were when we could not meet in person.

Q: How did your social relationships evolve during the pandemic?
R: Helping with homework on top of my own work was completely draining. We bought a second property in the countryside for weekends away. We even saved money during lockdown since we could not travel or eat out. Society proved more fragile than I thought but also more resilient.

Q: Did you find the restrictions justified or excessive?
R: Some people in my circle were hesitant, we had many long conversations. Wearing a mask became second nature after a few weeks.

Q: What lessons do you take from this period?
R: Online grocery shopping became a habit I have kept ever since. The mental health consequences are still with us, I think, even now.

Q: How do you look back on the vaccination campaign?
R: The pandemic barely affected our family budget, we were fortunate. I used some of our savings to get through the uncertain months.
